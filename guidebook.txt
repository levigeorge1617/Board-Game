Setup:
Choose a player to be the monster. 

All other players work as a team of heroes to defeat the monster player. 

Each hero player must choose their unique hero color.  

Only 1 of each color is allowed.  

All 5 colors must be used. 

The game can be played with just 2 players: 1 monster player and 1 hero player controlling 5 heroes. 

The monster player will roll the monster dice to choose what monster they play as. 

The monster player may setup all the buildings on the board, don't block any doors. 

The heroes may choose to move any 2 buildings of their choosing, Don't block any doors. 

The heroes place their starting platform anywhere on the board, including inside a building. 

The monster player rolls all the objective dice and place them with their rolled value anywhere on the board. 

The game begins with the hero's first turn. 



dice / board / buildings:
There are several kinds of Dice used in this game. 

 

Hero Dice: 

Each hero has a corresponding set of dice that match their hero color. There is Action, Movement, and Bonus Dice. See below for more details. 

 

Monster Dice: 

The monster has a set of black / grey dice that are used for various things. See below for more details. 

 

 

Action Dice ☼: 

These dice are used to perform actions OR move. 

Actions include reviving a dead hero, collecting an objective, attacking, or entering/leaving a building. Casting an ability takes actions. 

 

Movement Dice ֍: 

These dice can only be used to move spaces, they don't count towards actions. Some heroes can use movement rolls to do things. 

 

Bonus Dice  ☼/֍:

These dice CAN ONLY be used for the bonus they apply to, (IE: bonus movement֍ dice can only be used for movement֍).

They can't be used for combat or setting a Gravestone.

As the team collects objectives, EVERY hero gains the same rewards (this is why the hero cards no longer list them — it is a universal rule): an extra card is drawn at 2, 5 and 6 total objectives; the bonus movement֍ die unlocks at 4; and the bonus action☼ die unlocks at 7. Each hero's card only lists what is UNIQUE to that hero.

 

Objective Dice◆/◇: 

During the setup of the game, the monster player rolls the objective dice and places them at their choosing on the board.  

x◆: this icon means it can be done every turn, once the objective count requirement has been reached. 

X◇: this icon means it can be done only once, and triggers once the objective county requirement has been reached. 

 

Minion Dice: 

These dice indicate the health of a minion. 

 

Board: 

The board consists of a number / letter grid. A1 is considered the first space, A2 is the second space. 

 

Buildings are placed on the board by the player controlling the monster. 

Buildings are not allowed to be placed with any exit/door being blocked by the edge of the board or another building. 

 winning and objectives dice:
 How to win the game: 

Monster: Kill all Heroes. 

Heroes: Collect all Objective dice. 

 

Collecting Objectives: 

To collect an objective, the hero must be standing next to the objective and have line of sight of it(unless an ability says otherwise). 

Use action☼ dice rolls to reduce the objective to zero. When it reaches zero, collect the objective for the team and remove it from the board. 

 
 
 
 rules / events / actions:
 Objects: 

Anything that occupies a space on the board is considered an object. This includes, but is not limited to, things like objective dice, hero pieces, gravestones, minions, barriers, doors, pets, monster pieces. 

 

Monster's turn:

Draw a card to start the turn, then take any of these actions, in any order (some more than once — read the monster's card):

- Play monster cards.
- GRID-ROLL ATTACK: roll the grid to drop the monster on a random square, then Strike every hero it now SEES (within its sight, along a clear line). This is the monster's dangerous ranged attack. When you roll the grid, the monster CHOOSES one axis — a row or a column — and the other is rolled at random; some monsters roll BOTH at random (see their card).
- MOVE and attack: slide or step the monster across the board; if it ends with a hero within its REACH, it may Strike that hero. (Some monsters also apply their reach to a grid-roll landing.)
- Use its abilities (summon minions, place barriers, buffs, and so on).

Grid-roll attacks reach as far as the monster's SIGHT; move attacks reach as far as its REACH — both use the same combat (below). Monsters grow stronger as objectives are collected: more grid rolls, more moves, or bigger sight/reach (see each monster's x◆ line).

If a grid roll or a move lands the monster on a hero or pet, that piece dies and leaves a grave; the monster takes any adjacent space. If the monster enters a building a hero is in, that hero may Flee.



Reach & Sight — how far pieces attack and see:

REACH is how far a piece can ATTACK. It is counted as a SQUARE — a space in ANY direction, diagonals included — so a diagonal neighbour is 1 away and reach 2 covers the 5x5 block around the piece. Every fighter has a reach (1 for most; the Ranger reaches 3). Reach is a balance lever: a fighter you out-reach can't hit you back (see Combat).

Heroes attack anything within their reach — a hero's own line of sight does NOT matter. Only the MONSTER uses SIGHT: how far it can SEE heroes along a clear line, for its grid-roll attacks. Some monsters grow their sight or reach as objectives are collected.

MOVEMENT is counted differently — in straight steps (up/down/left/right); a diagonal takes 2 steps. The GREEN hero is the exception: GREEN steps diagonally for 1. On the board GREEN's outline shows a square and a diamond.

Line of sight (a clear line — for the monster's sight):

A clear line is blocked by building walls, doors, heroes, pets, minions, barriers, and objectives. GRAVESTONES do NOT block it, and the looking piece's own space and the target's space never block. A few monsters ignore cover — their sight passes through walls and objects.

In the app: right-click (or touch-and-hold) a piece to light up its reach (red) — and, for a monster, its sight (amber) — with a distance number on every enemy. With a piece lit, right-click / hold an empty square to print the shortest steps to walk there (✕ if walled off).

 

 

Heroes Dying: 

When a hero dies, they roll their action dice☼(not their bonus dice). That is how many actions☼ must be spent by an ally to revive them. 

Replace their hero piece with the action dice☼, making sure to have the correct number from the roll. This is now considered the hero's gravestone. 

 

Reviving a hero: 

To perform a revive, an ally hero must be standing next to the dead hero's action dice☼(gravestone) and have line of sight of it. 

That hero can use their action dice☼ rolls to reduce the action dice of the dead hero. 

When the dead hero's action dice☼ reaches zero, they are revived and the dice is replaced with the hero piece. Their health is set to 4 

The revived hero may take their turn this round, but can only use their movement dice֍. They may not perform any actions☼ or play cards. 

 

Minions:  

Minions are placed on board randomly. To summon a minion the monster rolls the minions action dice☼, this is its health.   

The monster uses an action dice☼ to move the minions. This can be divided between multiple minions. A minion sees (and strikes) only as far as its REACH — 1 space by default, counted as a square so diagonals count, more if the monster buffs it. On the monster's turn, minions can be moved to engage a hero for combat.

Minions should be moved BEFORE the monster's movement.



Pets (Hunter):

The Hunter may summon a pet — a hero-side piece (Attack 1 / Defense 1 / 3 life). A pet fights the monster's side (its minions, barriers and clones) and can be attacked by them. It shares the Hunter's movement rolls (not bonus dice) and flies over obstacles (but not walls). A pet is destroyed if it ever fights the MONSTER itself — keep it on minions. The monster's minion buffs (Oblex) never apply to a pet.



Combat:

Everyone — hero, monster, minion, pet — has an ATTACK number and a DEFENSE number of combat dice (each die: 3 skull ☠, 2 shield 🛡, 1 blank). Effects and modifier dice (from cards or abilities) may already sit on a fighter, or be applied.

A hero's deliberate attack COSTS 1 action☼ and needs the target within the hero's REACH (heroes ignore line of sight). A monster attacks by grid roll (using its sight) or by moving into reach — see the Monster's turn.

One attack is ONE exchange, resolved together:

1. Each side rolls its dice — the attacker its Attack, the defender its Defense — plus any modifier dice, and adds any flat base skulls/shields.
2. Tally each side's skulls and shields.
3. If a side's skulls beat the opponent's shields, the difference is dealt: a hero, minion or pet loses that much life; a MONSTER is REPELLED (pushed) that many spaces — heroes cannot kill a monster, only shove it.
4. REACH GATES THE COUNTER: the defender deals its skulls back ONLY if the attacker is within the defender's reach. A melee fighter struck from range can't hit back; a long-reach attacker (the Ranger, a wide-reach monster) strikes safely. A monster whose reach has grown past yours can't be pushed away — break line of sight or Flee instead.

No re-roll-until-death, no "who goes first." Attacks cost actions and resolve on your own turn, so combat can't loop. To end a fight, break away — a wall, a corner, a building, or a Flee.



Escaping (Flee):

Whenever a hero is Struck, its Defense roll does double duty: the shields block hits AND, if there are enough of them, let the hero flee. Each combat has a FLEE THRESHOLD — the shields needed to escape — and the app shows it on every roll.

The threshold starts at 2 shields and shifts with:
- the HERO — the Scout and Thief are nimble and flee on 1 fewer shield; heavy heroes (Paladin, Barbarian, Cleric) need 1 more,
- the MONSTER — some are harder to escape (Oblex's swarm, +1) or easier (Wyht, -1),
- the CIRCUMSTANCE — a building is a safe haven (flee into one freely); leaving one costs -2 life at the door.

You never need more shields than your Defense could show, so fleeing is always at least possible — a low-defense hero's threshold is capped at 1. If your Defense roll shows at least the threshold in shields, you may move out of the attacker's reach and line and end the fight. The most reliable escape is still positional: get behind a wall or into a building so the monster can't see or reach you.

 
 
 cards:
 There are two decks of cards, the hero cards (white) and the monster cards(black). Place them in their own stack as two draw piles. 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Drawing Cards:  

At the start of the hero's turn, all heroes draw a card from the hero draw pile. 

At the start of the monster's turn, it draws a card from the monster draw pile. 

 

Hero's Cards can be used at any time during the hero's turn. 

Monster's Cards can be used during the monster's turn before moving. 

 

Hand size: 

Each player is limited to 10 cards. 

If you draw a card and go over 10 in your hand, choose a card to discard to remain at 10 in hand. 

 

Draw Piles: 

New cards are drawn here. If a draw pile becomes empty, shuffle the discard pile back into the draw pile. 

Hero's draw from the White draw pile 

Monster draws from the Black draw pile. 

 

Discard Piles: 

Any used card should go to respective discard pile unless otherwise stated. 

 

Corrupted Potion: 

If this card is drawn in the hero's draw pile; discard it, and the monster draws a card from the monster draw pile. 

 

Playing a card: 

Pay the Action☼ or Movement֍ cost in the bottom left corner. 0 means it costs nothing. Resolve the effects of the card, and then place it in the appropriate discard pile.
